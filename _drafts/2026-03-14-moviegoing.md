TL;DR: It costs $78.17 for an adult and two kids to go to the movies, including snacks. This includes "value coupons". Hence why I go to the movies a maximum of once a year.

I bought two Kid's Movie Packs from Costco ($13.99 each) and a General Admission ticket for a movie at Landmark Cinemas this weekend. The total came to $30.79, which is a lot more than I expected. Here's the breakdown:

Costco Kid Movie Pack 1 x $3.39 = $3.39
Costco Kid Movie Pack 1 x $3.39 = $3.39
General Admission Ticket 1 x $18.25 = $18.25
Online Booking Fee $3.00
Subtotal $28.03
Applicable Tax $3.54
Order Total $30.79

$30.79 plus the two kids combo tickets from Costco, which are $13.99 each, means that the total cost for an adult and two kids to go to the movies is $58.17.

Try to understand each ticket:
Premiere 3D Shout Out CC DVS Recliner
Premiere EXTRA 2D Shout Out CC DVS Recliner
Premiere 2D Shout Out CC DVS Recliner
2D Shout Out CC DVS Recliner

Can't I just go to a movie? Why are there so many options?

I'll get a combo for myself, which will add at least another $20, so the total is minimum $78.17 for an adult and two kids to go to the movies, including snacks.

What's the alternative? Streaming at home costs nothing. A snack splurge at home is maybe $10 for a family of three. So the total cost for a movie night at home is $10, compared to $78.17 at the theater. Are you getting 8 times the value at the theater?
